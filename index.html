What is FPL?

Author: Shaun Malazarte

Fantasy Premier League (FPL) is an online football game that allows participants to act as virtual team managers, creating and managing their own fantasy football teams using real-life players from the English Premier League (EPL). Each player in the Premier League is assigned a value, and participants are given a budget (typically £100 million) to build a team of 15 players. This team consists of 11 starting players and four substitutes, with participants required to select players from different positions: goalkeepers, defenders, midfielders, and forwards.

The aim of FPL is to score the highest possible points based on the real-world performances of the selected players in actual EPL matches. Points are awarded for actions like scoring goals, providing assists, keeping clean sheets, and making saves, while points are deducted for actions like conceding goals or receiving red or yellow cards. Managers can also choose a captain each week, whose points are doubled.

One of the key aspects of FPL is the ability to make transfers, allowing managers to adjust their squads in response to player form, injuries, or favorable matchups. Managers are given one free transfer per game week, with the option to roll over unused transfers or use additional transfers at a cost of points.

In Fantasy Premier League (FPL), chips play a crucial role in helping managers boost their team’s performance in specific game weeks. These strategic tools allow for flexibility and enable managers to maximize their points when used effectively. There are four main chips available to FPL managers: the Wildcard, Triple Captain, Bench Boost, and Free Hit.


  1. The Wildcard chip allows managers to make unlimited transfers without incurring any points penalties. This is particularly useful when a team needs a complete overhaul. Each manager is given two Wildcards per season: one in the first half and another in the second half.
  2. The Triple Captain chip offers a chance for a major points haul by tripling the score of the selected captain for one game week. Since captains normally receive double points.
  3. The Bench Boost chip is designed to maximize the contributions of the entire squad. When activated, it counts the points of all 15 players, including those on the bench, instead of just the starting 11.
  4. The Free Hit chip provides the flexibility to make unlimited transfers for a single game week. After the game week ends, the squad reverts to its previous state.

Fantasy Premier League is not only a game of strategy and prediction but also one of constant engagement and adaptation. Players must stay updated on injuries, team news, and fixtures to make informed decisions. With its blend of real-life football action and strategic gameplay, FPL has grown into a global phenomenon, fostering a large and active community of fans who share tips, analyses, and banter throughout the season.
